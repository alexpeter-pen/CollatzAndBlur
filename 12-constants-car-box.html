The constants, at a glance

  • \(\rho\) ‚Äî the switch budget for estimating the tiny additive term \(\varepsilon(n)=\log(1+1/(3n))\) inside a multiplicative (log) lens after a short local blur.
  • \(\delta\) ‚Äî the drift margin guaranteed by the residue inequalities for \(V(n)=\log n + \varphi(n\bmod 2^k)\).

They appear together in the certificate. A typical target is to make \(\delta>0\) with \(\rho+\delta<\log(4/3)\) (or another explicit threshold) so one odd step contracts the scale.

Boxcar (‚Äúcar‚Äëbox‚Äù) window

Pick a radius \(m\) and set uniform, positive weights \(w_h=\tfrac{1}{2m+1}\) for \(h\in\{-m,\ldots,m\}\). For a function on odd residues \(f\), define the local blur

\((\mathcal B f)(r) \,=\, \sum\limits_{h=-m}^m w_h\, f(r+2h) \quad (\bmod\ 2^k).\)

  • Locality: only nearby last bits mix (odd residues differ by 2).
  • Positivity: \(w_h\ge 0\) keeps the bound conservative.
  • Finite: everything lives mod \(2^k\); no tails.

Other windows (e.g., Fej√©r) are possible; boxcar is simplest and works well here.

How \(\rho\) and \(\delta\) are obtained

  1. Choose \(k\), window radius \(m\), and weights (boxcar by default).
  2. Compute \(\overline D(r)\) and the residue graph \(r\mapsto r'\) (treat \(r^*\) with \(v_2\ge k\)).
  3. Bound \(\varepsilon(n)\) by \(\rho\) on each residue class via interval arithmetic (after blur).
  4. Solve the LP maximizing \(\delta\) subject to all residue inequalities and a gauge for \(\varphi\) (e.g., mean zero).
  5. Verify every inequality independently with directed‚Äërounding intervals.

Trade‚Äëoff: larger \(m\) smooths \(\overline D\) (helping \(\delta\)) but may enlarge \(\rho\).

Why these choices fit the problem

  • Addition vs multiplication: \(\rho\) prices the unavoidable lens switch; \(\delta\) captures genuine average contraction.
  • Boxcar positivity: prevents ‚Äúcheating‚Äù by cancellation‚Äîessential for a trustworthy certificate.
  • Discrete derivative: \(\varphi(r')-\varphi(r)\) is exactly what the inequality controls; it‚Äôs the right notion of slope on the residue graph.
