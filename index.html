Welcome

The Collatz map mixes multiplication and addition in one step: \(n\mapsto 3n+1\) then divide by powers of two. These two operations live in different “lenses” (Mellin/log for ×, Fourier/linear for +). Demanding perfect sharpness in both leads to jagged behavior at certain residues. Our approach is to pay a tiny, explicit toll \(\rho\) for switching lenses and use a short, positive local blur to stabilize the spikes. The payoff is a finite system of inequalities—an auditable certificate of uniform one‑step decrease for a Lyapunov scale \(V\).

No heavy prerequisites are required beyond basic number theory, valuations, and familiarity with Lyapunov ideas.

How to read this site

  • Proceed in order; each page is short and focused.
  • MathJax is included on every page—inline math like \(v_2(3n+1)\) and display equations render automatically.
  • Whenever we say “blur”, we mean a positive, local averaging on last bits; the constant \(\rho\) tracks its uniform effect.
